Planets

From rocky worlds to gas giants, planets reveal the diversity of solar systems.

Terrestrial

Rocky worlds

MercuryVenusEarthMars

Small, dense, with solid surfaces. They form close to stars where heat strips volatile gases, leaving rock and metal.

Gas & ice giants

Massive atmospheres

JupiterSaturnUranusNeptune

Rich in hydrogen, helium, and ices. These giants host extreme winds, rings, and magnetic fields that shape their systems.

Moons

From cratered relics to oceans beneath ice—mini worlds with big surprises.

Ocean worlds

Europa and Enceladus may harbor subsurface oceans warmed by tidal forces—prime targets in the search for life.

Ice shell: Kilometers thick, shielding liquid water below.
Plumes: Jets of water vapor venting from the surface.
Habitability: Energy and chemistry could support microbes.

Earth’s Moon

Our Moon stabilizes Earth’s tilt, drives tides, and preserves a record of solar system history on its ancient surface.

Mare & highlands: Dark basaltic plains and bright anorthosite terrain.
Regolith: Fine dust shaped by impacts and solar wind.
Tidal effects: Ocean tides and subtle crustal flexing on Earth.

Rovers

Robotic explorers turn distant worlds into places we can read and understand.

Mars

Perseverance

Collects rock cores, searches for biosignatures, and tests oxygen production from Martian atmosphere.

Legacy

Curiosity

Analyzes minerals and organics, revealing a once-habitable lake environment in Gale Crater.

Moon

Artemis era

New lunar rovers aim to map ice in shadowed craters and support human surface missions.

Space operations

Launch, communicate, navigate, and protect—operations turn missions into successes.

Launch systems

From expendable rockets to reusable boosters, launch vehicles deliver payloads to orbit and beyond.

Staging: Shed mass for efficiency as speed increases.
Guidance: Avionics keep trajectories precise through ascent.
Recovery: Reusability reduces cost and increases cadence.

Mission control

Telemetry, tracking, and command networks keep craft connected while teams orchestrate maneuvers and troubleshooting.

Deep space network: Giant antennas receive faint signals across the solar system.
Navigation: Radiometric and optical methods refine position and velocity.
Operations: Sequencing instruments, power, and communications windows.

Space telescopes

Observatories above the atmosphere capture light across the spectrum.

Optical

Hubble Space Telescope

From stunning galaxies to measuring cosmic expansion, Hubble’s legacy reshaped modern astronomy.

Infrared

Infrared observatories

Infrared light pierces dust to reveal star nurseries, exoplanet atmospheres, and the earliest galaxies.

Space theories

Ideas that frame our cosmic story—from beginnings to fate.

Big Bang and cosmic expansion

The universe expanded from a hot, dense state. Matter cooled, structures formed, and light stretched—seen in background radiation and redshift.

Dark matter and dark energy

Unseen mass shapes galaxies, while a mysterious energy drives accelerated expansion—together dominating the cosmic budget.

Planet formation

Disks around young stars birth planets. Dust clumps, gas accretes, and migrations sculpt diverse system architectures.

Life beyond Earth

Habitability depends on energy, chemistry, and stability. Ocean worlds and exoplanets offer promising laboratories.

Asteroids

Primitive building blocks and occasional hazards—asteroids hold clues to the solar system’s origins.

Near-Earth

Near-Earth objects

Asteroids with orbits that cross Earth’s path are tracked for planetary defense and studied for resources and science.

Main belt

Between Mars and Jupiter

The main belt hosts diverse bodies—carbon-rich, stony, and metallic—preserving early solar materials.
